Current determination

Statewide enabling is HRS §46-80.5 (Special improvement district), allowing charter counties to create SIDs by ordinance for supplemental services and improvements funded by special assessments. These are the legal BID equivalents. Honolulu currently implements four districts under ROH Ch. 28: No. 1 Waikīkī Business Improvement District (2000), No. 2 Fort Street Mall expanded and renamed Downtown Honolulu Business Improvement District by Ord. 25-47 (2025), No. 3 Waikīkī Beach Special Improvement District (2015), and No. 4 Waikīkī Transportation Management Special Improvement District (2017). Hawaiʻi County Code Ch. 35 has active Kailua Village BID No. 1; proposed Downtown Hilo BID No. 2 was rejected in 2023. Maui has enabling authority under Ch. 3.70 but no active district has yet been confirmed; Kauai planning material says the county had not authorized one. The atlas now maps all five verified active districts. Downtown Honolulu and Kailua Village boundaries were reconstructed from ordinance assessment parcel lists using current State parcel geometry; the three overlapping Waikīkī boundaries are generalized from official ordinance maps. No statewide registry or unified official GIS exists, so coverage remains substantial rather than comprehensive until negative findings for Maui and Kauai receive direct county confirmation.

Next action

Confirm through Maui and Kauai county clerk, finance, and assessment records that no active districts have been established under their enabling authorities; monitor Honolulu Chapter 28 and Hawaiʻi County Chapter 35 for changes. Five active districts are now mapped: Honolulu SIDs Nos. 1-4 and Kailua Village BID No. 1.

Current determination

Illinois authorizes classic BIDs under Business Improvement District Law (65 ILCS 125/, P.A. 103-646, eff. 7-1-24), limited to municipalities >500,000 population (Chicago only). Chicago DPD accepts feasibility applications for BIDs (deadline Nov 1, 2026 for 2027 start); none active as of research (law recent; BIDs cannot overlap SSAs). Longstanding statewide equivalent is Special Service Areas under Special Service Area Tax Law (35 ILCS 200/27-5 et seq. / Article 27 of Property Tax Code), available to municipalities and counties for special services/improvements (including downtown/business corridor marketing, security, cleaning, beautification) funded by property tax levies solely on the SSA. Creation via local ordinance after notice, hearing, petition/objection process; maps/ordinances filed with county clerks/recorders. Chicago has ~58 active SSAs with authoritative provider lists, citywide maps, and downloadable GIS boundaries. Examples confirmed elsewhere (Peoria, Lake/Cook/Peoria/Kendall Counties GIS, Moline, Highland Park business SSAs, Aurora, Oak Park, Evanston, Yorkville, etc.). No central public statewide registry or inventory of all SSAs/BIDs located (IDOR has historically tracked 600+ for tax extension/property tax purposes as of ~2011, generating tens of millions annually, but no public list/GIS). Distinguish from unrelated 'Business Districts' under Illinois Municipal Code 65 ILCS 5/11-74.3 (sales-tax increment redevelopment of blighted areas). Coverage authoritative/complete for Chicago; partial for rest of state via scattered county GIS and municipal pages. Failed searches do not prove absence. Enabling URLs: BID https://www.ilga.gov/Legislation/ILCS/Articles?ActID=4518&ChapterID=14 ; SSA article/sections via https://www.ilga.gov/legislation/ILCS/details?MajorTopic=&Chapter=&ActName=Property%20Tax%20Code.&ActID=596&ChapterID=8&ChapAct=35+ILCS+200%2F&SeqStart=86400000&SeqEnd=89400000 and https://ilga.gov/documents/legislation/ilcs/documents/003502000K27-5.htm .

Current determination

Enabling statute is M.G.L. c. 40O (Business Improvement Districts), enacted ~1994; BIDs are contiguous areas ≥75% commercial/industrial/retail/mixed-use, formed by petition of ≥60% of property owners representing ≥51% assessed valuation, local hearing/approval, and filing with EOED. Official Mass.gov page and manual confirm process and that petitions are forwarded to EOED for technical assistance/compliance, but no public statewide registry, inventory, or GIS layer of all BIDs was located (failed searches are not proof of absence). Local term is consistently 'Business Improvement District' or 'BID'; distinguish carefully from Cultural Districts (Mass Cultural Council), Parking Benefit Districts, Tourism Destination Marketing Districts (TDMDs), District Improvement Financing (DIF), historic districts, and voluntary Main Street-style organizations (toolkit explicitly separates these). Known/operating examples (from official toolkit, case studies, district sites, and secondary confirmations such as ~10-11 total cited in Hyannis/New Bedford sources) include: Downtown Boston (Alliance), Greenway BID (Boston), Newmarket (Boston), Amherst, Central Square (Cambridge), Hyannis Main Street, Hudson, Springfield, Worcester, Taunton; Edgartown has public ArcGIS BID boundary (status may be active/proposed). No statewide map/GeoJSON found; local GIS/boundaries available for several. Secondary analyses (e.g. MassINC) use MassGIS parcels to examine select BIDs. Coverage remains partial pending authoritative full list or exhaustive municipal sweep.

Next action

Contact EOED BID staff (elizabeth.hahn@mass.gov) for any internal inventory of petitions/established BIDs; systematically check remaining municipalities and MassGIS/open data portals for additional boundary layers; verify status of Edgartown/Athol and any post-2024 formations; cross-check municipal ordinances and assessor data for complete local coverage.

Current determination

Enabling statute is Chapter 35.87A RCW (Parking and Business Improvement Areas), authorizing cities/towns/counties to form special assessment districts for economic development, parking, promotion, maintenance, security, etc., via petition (typically ~60% of assessments) or resolution. Locally termed PBIAs/BIAs/BIDs; Tourism Improvement Areas (e.g., Seattle STIA) also use this authority. Authoritative statewide registry or complete inventory not located (MRSC provides examples only). Distinguish carefully from unrelated Local Improvement Districts (LIDs under other RCW chapters, e.g., capital infrastructure assessments) and Public Development Authorities. Multiple active districts confirmed in Seattle (11+ including Metropolitan Improvement District/MID and others), Tacoma (Downtown BIA with GIS), Spokane (Downtown and East Sprague), Richland (2), Olympia, Snohomish, Everett, Yakima, Aberdeen, etc. Municipal GIS available for major cities; many boundaries also in ordinances or PDF maps. No proof of absence elsewhere—coverage incomplete without exhaustive municipal search.
